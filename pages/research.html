James D Pickering

Research

My research falls into two categories - "spectroscopy and dynamics", and "chemical education". I am a member of both the Biological Spectroscopy group and the Research and Innovation in Chemical Education group in the School of Chemistry, University of Leicester.

I currently have a fully funded PhD position available in the area of ultrafast protein dynamics. This is a collaborative project with Dr Philip Ash from the Leicester Biological Spectroscopy group, and other collaborators from the Leicester Institute for Structural and Chemical Biology (LISCB). The plan is to use ultrafast nonlinear spectroscopy to look at vibrational dynamics of heme proteins, to better understand the fundamental mechanisms behind key biological processes such as oxygen transport. This project is highly interdisciplinary and so applications from anyone with a physical or life sciences background are welcomed. Please get in contact with any informal or formal enquiries!

I am interested in the interaction of light and matter, and particularly how a deep understanding of the complex physics of light interaction can be a powerful tool for revealing information about complex chemical systems. I also work extensively in developing user-friendly computational tools for scientists who process large and complex datasets.

I am also actively working in science education, and developing novel tools and methodologies to help students appreciate the physical sides of chemistry in new ways.

Details of my publications are available on Google Scholar.
